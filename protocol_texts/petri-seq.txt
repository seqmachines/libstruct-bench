PETRI-seq Detailed Protocol
Related to ”Prokaryotic single-cell RNA sequencing by in situ combinatorial indexing”
Sydney B. Blattman, Wenyan Jiang, Panos Oikonomou, Saeed Tavazoie
Last Updated: February 10, 2021
Some Notes Before Starting
• Before proceeding with the full protocol below, we recommend trying in situ reverse transcription and
qPCR to confirm cell preparation is working. See paper for details (”qPCR quantification after in situ
DNase or in situ RT” section in methods)
• PETRI-seq cell preparation and split-pool barcoding can be done in 2 days, as detailed below. Please
be aware that day 2 takes about 12 hours and does not include any stopping points prior to lysis. We
will update this protocol if we later find any feasible stopping points.
• Please go through the materials listed below, and prepare reagents as needed. Specifically, we rec-
ommend diluting and annealing the barcoding oligos in the first half of day 2. We usually do this during
the many centrifugations.

Overview of Workflow
Cell Preparation
Day 1
Note: Cells should be grown on Day 1 to the desired growth phase, which may require advanced prepa-
ration on preceding days depending on the experiment. For example, in our publication, we started E. coli
and S. aureus overnights on the day before, then diluted cells into fresh media on ”Day 1”, and grew the
cells to exponential or stationary phase. The following steps begin with cells that are in the growth phase
and condition of interest.
1. Transfer ~9.6*108 cells (calculated roughly based on 1 OD600 = 8*108 cells/mL) to a centrifuge tube
(room temperature) and spin at 5,525g for 2 minutes at 4◦C.
• The number of cells can be varied, but we recommend a corresponding adjustment of volumes
used throughout the cell preparation protocol. For example, if double the number of cells are
taken, then fix cells in 6 mL formaldehyde instead of 3 mL (and so on for the remaining steps). In
our paper, we used ~2.2*109 cells fixed in 7 mL, which results in many more cells than needed
for split-pool barcoding.
• If combining multiple cell types for fixation (as we did with stationary-phase RFP E. coli and
exponential-phase GFP E. coli), measure the OD600 of these cultures, then combine them to
reach ~9.6*108 cells total (in the desired ratio of cell types).
2. Take out tubes immediately after spin, and keep cold on ice. Remove supernatant.
3. Resuspend cells in 3 mL of ice-cold 4% formaldehyde.
4. Rotate cell suspension for ~16 hours at 4◦C.
• Using something like a Thermo Scientific Lab Quake Rotator, for example.

Day 2
** Keep cells on ice as much as possible (unless otherwise indicated) throughout Day 2 and chill all reagents
on ice before use
** For large volumes, we centrifuge at 5,525g using Allegra 25R centrifuge (Beckman Coulter); for small
volumes, we centrifuge at 7,000g using 5415D centrifuge (Eppendorf)
1. Centrifuge cells at 5,525g for 10 minutes at 4◦C.
2. Remove supernatant, and resuspend cells in 3 mL PBS-RI.
3. Centrifuge cells at 5,525g for 10 minutes at 4◦C.
4. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 300µL PBS-RI.
5. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
6. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 150µL PBS-RI. Add 150µL 100% ethanol and invert tube
to mix.
7. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
8. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 300µL PBS-RI (wash #1).
9. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
10. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 300µL PBS-RI (wash #2).
11. Resuspend cells in the appropriate cell wall degrading enzyme.
• Lysozyme: 45µL of 100µg/mL lysozyme in TEL-RI
• Lysostaphin: 45µL of 40µg/mL lysostaphin in TEL-RI
• Notes: We have validated lysozyme for E. coli permeabilization and lysostaphin for S. aureus
permeabilization. We have only used the enzymes separately (i.e. E. coli prepared alone or S.
aureus prepared alone). The lysozyme concentration was set after titrating from 4 to 500µg/mL
with little change in cDNA yield (measured by qPCR).
12. Permeabilize cells for 15 minutes at room temperature.
13. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
14. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 75µL PBS-RI (wash #1).
15. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
16. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 75µL PBS-RI (wash #2).
17. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
18. Resuspend cells in 30µL DNase-RI buffer.
19. Add 3µL DNase I
20. Incubate cells at room temperature for 30 minutes
21. Add 3µL DNase I stop solution
22. Heat cells to 50◦C for 10 minutes with shaking at 500 rpm
• We use a multi-therm shaker for this
• DNase I is typically inactivated at 70◦C, but we inactivate at 50◦C to preserve cell integrity
23. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 8 minutes at 4◦C.
• The cell pellet usually looks different after DNase inactivation, specifically more pale and smaller
than before DNase. While the pellet should remain visible, this difference in appearance is to be
expected.
24. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 75µL PBS-RI (wash #1).

Split-Pool Barcoding
Day 2 (contd.)
1. Using a multi-channel pipette, aliquot 2µL of each Round 1 RT oligo (10µM) across a 96-well plate.
• We typically seal the plate with a foil seal at this point and centrifuge the plate to ensure oligos
go to bottom of tubes.
2. Prepare an RT reaction mix (total volume 960µL) by combining the following:
• 240µL 5x Maxima H Minus RT buffer
• 24µL dNTPs
• 12µL SUPERase In RNase Inhibitor
• 24µL Maxima H Minus Reverse Transcriptase
• (660 - vc) µL RNase-free water
• vc µL cells (3*107 cells)
– If multiple cell types are to be combined at this stage, add a total of 3*107 cells (e.g. 1.5*107
S. aureus and 1.5*107 E. coli)
3. Add 8µL of the RT reaction mix to each well of the 96-well plate containing the Round 1 RT oligos
(already containing 2µL oligos)
• We typically aliquot 106µL of the RT reaction mix across an 8-strip PCR tube then use a multi-
channel pipette to aliquot 8µL into each well of the 96-well plate. To avoid cells pelleting in the
PCR plate, we do not centrifuge the plate after adding cells. Instead, we try to add cells directly
to the bottom of the plate and pipette up and down a few times.
4. Incubate the PCR plate for RT as follows:
• 50◦C 10 minutes
• 8◦C 12 seconds
• 15◦C 45 seconds
• 20◦C 45 seconds
• 30◦C 30 seconds
• 42◦C 6 minutes
• 50◦C 16 minutes
• 4◦C hold
5. Immediately after RT finishes and reaches 4◦C, pool reactions into one eppendorf tube. Be sure to
pipette up and down in cell well to get all of the cells.
6. Add 5% Tween-20 to the pooled cells, reaching a final concentration of 0.04% (125x dilution)
• The volume of 5% Tween-20 to add should be calculated after measuring the volume of recovered
pooled cells. For example, if the volume of pooled cells after RT is 830µL, add 6.6µL 5% Tween-
20. We split the pooled cells (before adding Tween-20) into a few tubes so the volume in each is
less than ~350µL. This is important for the dilution in the next step.
Sydney Blattman, Tavazoie Lab at Columbia University

7. Incubate cells in 0.04% Tween-20 on ice for 3 minutes.
8. Add PBS-RI to bring the final concentration of Tween-20 to 0.01%.
• For example, add a total of 2510µL PBS-RI to 836.6µL of 0.04% PBS-RI. If 836.6µL is split into
3 tubes of 279µL, then add 836.6µL PBS-RI to each of these tubes.
9. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 10-20 minutes at 4◦C. A small pellet should be visible.
• We were routinely centrifuging for 20 minutes (and sometimes at 10,000g), but we have noticed
that cells pellet sooner than 20 minutes, so it should be fine to centrifuge for less time if a pellet
is visible. We also often remove most of the supernatant and then spin an additional few minutes
before removing the rest of it.
10. During centrifugation, aliquot 2.24µL of annealed Round 2 Ligation Oligos into a new 96-well plate.
Briefly centrifuge the plate to collect volume at the bottom.
11. After centrifugation, remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 600µL 1x T4 ligase buffer supple-
mented with 0.1 U/µL SUPERase In RNase Inhibitor.
12. Prepare a ligation master mix as follows:
• 7.5µL RNase-free water
• 37.5µL 10x T4 ligase buffer
• 16.7µL SUPERase In RNase Inhibitor
• 5.6µL BSA
• 27.9µL T4 ligase
• 600µL resuspended cells
13. Add 5.76µL of the RT reaction mix to each well of the 96-well plate containing the Round 2 ligation
oligos (already containing 2.24µL oligos)
• We typically aliquot 76µL of the ligation reaction mix across an 8-strip of PCR tubes then use a
multi-channel pipette to aliquot 5.76µL into each well of the 96-well plate. To avoid cells pelleting
in the PCR plate, we do not centrifuge the plate after adding cells. Instead, we try to add cells
directly to the bottom of the plate and pipette up and down a few times.
14. Incubate ligations for 30 minutes at 37◦C.
15. After the ligation, add 2µL Round 2 blocking mix to each well.
• Round 2 blocking mix includes:
– 37.5µL 400µM SB84 blocking oligo (annealed to self, see explanation in materials)
– 37.5µL 400µM SB85 blocking oligo
– 25µL 10x T4 ligase buffer
– 150µL RNase-free water
• We typically aliquot 27µL of blocking mix across an 8-strip of PCR tubes then use a multi-channel
to aliquot 2µL into each well
16. Incubate the blocking reaction for 30 minutes at 37◦C.
17. During blocking, aliquot 3.49µL of annealed Round 3 Ligation Oligos into a new 96-well plate. Briefly
centrifuge the plate to collect volume at the bottom.
18. Pool cells after blocking into a single eppendorf tube.
19. Add the following to the pooled cells for the Round 3 ligation:
• 46µL 10x T4 ligase buffer
• 12.65µL T4 ligase

• We typically aliquot 110µL of the ligation reaction mix across an 8-strip of PCR tubes then use a

22. After the ligation, add 10µL Round 3 blocking mix to each well.
• Round 3 blocking mix includes:
– 72µL 400µM SB81 blocking oligo (annealed to self, see explanation in materials)
– 72µL 400µM SB82 blocking oligo
– 120µL 10x T4 ligase buffer
– 336µL RNase-free water
– 600µL 0.5 M EDTA
• We typically aliquot 133µL of blocking mix across an 8-strip of PCR tubes then use a multi-channel
to aliquot 10µL into each well
23. After adding the blocking mix, pool cells (total volume ~2.1 mL) and split into 2 eppendorf tubes.
24. Measure the volume of pooled cells, and add Tween-20 to a final concentration of 0.01%.
25. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 10 minutes at 4◦C.
26. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 500µL TEL-RI + 0.01% Tween-20.
• The cells in the two tubes can be combined here in a total of 500µL.
27. Centrifuge cells at 7,000g for 10 minutes at 4◦C.
28. Remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 30µL TEL-RI.
29. Count cells using a semen test hemocytometer.
30. Transfer ~10,000 cells into eppendorf tubes for lysis.
• Up to 40,000 cells can be taken for each lysate, but we typically take 10,000 cells in case cell
counts are underestimated. We usually prepare 10-20 lysates from the pooled cells at this stage
to be safe in case anything goes wrong in downstream steps or to be able to sequence more cells
as needed.
• We recommend counting cells and aliquoting lysates quickly, as cell lysis may occur prior to
making lysates and result in higher levels of cross-contamination in the final library. We also may
later recommend more washes, but at this point we have only tested a single wash before lysis.
31. Add TEL-RI to the 10,000 cells to bring the volume to 25µL.
32. Add 25µL 2x lysis-T and 5µL proteinase K (20 mg/mL) to the cells in TEL-RI.
33. Lyse cells for 1 hour at 55◦C with shaking at 750 rpm.
• We use a multi-therm shaker for this.
34. Save lysates at -80◦C.

Library Preparation
Day 3
Note that during library preparation, it is critical to carry over the entire library at each step before amplifica-
tion. Any lost cDNA prior to amplification will correspond to a reduction in UMIs captured per cell.
1. Thaw lysates on ice
2. Purify lysates by adding AMPure XP beads at a 1.8x ratio (approximately 99µL of beads, but measure
the volume of lysate to be precise). Elute in 20µL RNase-free water after following the purifications
steps below.
3. We use the following AMPure purification protocol:
(a) Incubate the lysate with beads for at least 5 minutes
(b) Place tubes on a magnetic stand.
(c) Let sit for about 2 minutes to allow beads to separate.
(d) Once the solution is clear, remove the liquid, leaving behind the beads and being very careful not
to remove any beads
(e) Add 200µL 80% ethanol and let sit for at least 30 seconds, keeping tubes on magnetic rack (wash
#1).
(f) Remove ethanol.
(g) Add 200µL 80% ethanol and let sit for at least 30 seconds, keeping tubes on magnetic rack (wash
#2).
(h) Remove ethanol.
(i) Keeping tubes on magnetic rack with lids open, allow to dry for ~10 minutes, watching to be sure
beads are not over-dried.
(j) Take tubes off magnetic rack, and resuspend beads in 20µL RNase-free water.
(k) Incubate beads in water for ~10 minutes to elute cDNA.
(l) Place tubes on the magnetic stand.
(m) Let sit for ~1 minutes, until solution is clear.
(n) Remove the entire volume, leaving behind all of the beads, and transfer the volume to a PCR
tube. We find that it is possible to recover the entire volume while excluding beads if pipetting
carefully.
4. Add the following mix to each purified lysate:
• 14µL RNase-free water
• 4µL NEBNext Second Strand Synthesis Reaction Buffer
• 2µL NEBNext Second Strand Synthesis Enzyme Mix
5. Incubate the 40µL reaction for 2.5 hours at 16◦C with heated lid off.
6. Purify the reaction by adding 72µL AMPure XP beads and following the purification protocol above.
Elute in 20µL water.
7. Proceed to tagmentation or store the double-stranded cDNA at -20◦C.
8. For tagmentation, we use a slightly modified version of the Nextera XT protocol.
9. Add 25µL TD buffer and 5µL ATM to the purified lysate.
