Smart-seq2 Version 6.1 (SS2-M)
INTRODUCTION
The aim of this protocol is to apply a modified version of Smart-Seq2 (Picelli S, et al. Nat Protocols, 2014) to obtain
full-length transcriptomic data from single cells.
Until amplification is done, always use low-retention Rainin pipette tips.

Sorting samples
1. Request FACS time with the Broad's flow core (calendar: Astrios) and specify the quote number for billing.

2. Working in a fume hood, aliquot freshly prepared 5 ul of Buffer TCL containing 1% 2-Mercaptoethanol into
96-well plates.
3. Seal plates with Microseal 'F' foils, barcode plates with Regev Lab stickers, label plates with sharpie, and
spin down. Keep at RT until sorting.
Some people sort. in 10 pl and it is necessary to know how much lysis buffer is used (as this affects
the volume of SPRI beads required).
4. Sort samples into 96-well plates. Immediately spin at 2000 RPM for up to 1 min and flash freeze on dry ice.
Store plates indefinitely at -80°C.
Iti is very helpful to sort a no-cell control into wells H11 and H12, and a positive control of 10-20
cells into wells A1 and. A2.

5. RNaseZap all work surfaces and pipettes (except multi-channel pipettes). Let sit - 3-5 min, then wipe down
and re-spray everything with 70% ethanol. Let sit - 3-5 min and wipe clean.
6. Start thermal cycler program (but hit PAUSE after START) to ensure lid temperature rises to 105°C.
7. Equilibrate RNA-SPRI beads to RT ( 30 min).
8. Make fresh 80% ethanol.
9. Prepare/open pipette tip boxes So that they are ready for use.
10. Thaw out reagents used for Mix 1 and Mix 2 (excluding enzymes).
You can prepare Mix 1 and Mix 2 at this time, adding everything except for reverse transcriptase.
Keep stored on ice until time of use.
11. Thaw cell lysate samples on ice. Once thawed, spin down.
It is important to know how much lysate volume exists per well, as this determines the amount of
SPRI beads to use. We commonly work with a lysate volume of 5 pl.
12. Vortex RNA-SPRI beads and pipette the amount to be used into a trough.
Remove the total amount of SPRI beads required with a serological pipette and place into trough.
This helps minimize contamination that can result from re-dipping the pipette in and out of the stock
solution.
13. Working at RT, add 2.2X RNA-SPRI beads (i.e. 11 pli if 5 pl of lysate). Gently and SLOWLY mix - 5 times.
Do not form bubbles during the mixing process (bubbles on the top of the surface are okay). The
presence of bubbles minimizes the interaction between beads and DNA/RNA molecules and will
result. in less capture off nucleotides.

Purify the WTA products
The WTA is our whole transcriptome amplified product. In this portion of the protocol, a smaller volume of SPRI
beads is used to exclude smaller nucleotide fragments (i.e. primer-dimers and off-target amplicons). The 70% ethanol
is important here (as opposed to 80% ethanol) because it helps solubilize CDNA, thereby making it harder to retain
the small primer dimers on the beads.
31. Equilibrate DNA-SPRI beads to RT (-30 min).
32. Spin plate down after removing from thermal cycler.
33. Vortex DNA-SPRI beads and pipette the amount to be used into a trough.
34. At RT, add 0.7X DNA-SPRI beads (i.e. 17.5 pl into each 25 pl-existing well) and mix a 10 times.
Nucleotide fragments are pushed out of the PEG solution and adhere to the SPRI beads in a
length-dependent manner (i.e. longer DNA fragments precipitate out preferentially and adhere to
beads first), and SO using SPRI bead concentrations less than 1X excludes smaller fragments (i.e.
- < 200 bp) from adhering to beads.
35. Cover and incubate at RT for 5 min.
36. Incubate on magnet for 6 min at RT.
37. With plate on magnet, gently aspirate and discard supernatant. Leave about 10 ul of supernatant left in each
well.
38. With plate on magnet, add 100 pl of 70% ethanol. Let sit a 30 sec.
39. Remove 100 pl of solution and repeat ethanol wash. Let sit - 30 sec.
40. After second wash, remove and discard supernatant.
41. Use the p20 multi-channel pipette to ensure that all ethanol is removed.
42. Keep plate on magnet and slightly covered. Dry out at RT for a 3-4 min.
43. Gently remove plate from magnet and gently resuspend in 20 pl of TE buffer. Let sit for - 1 min.
Once TE buffer is added, the WTA products dissolve into solution. We now care about the
supernatant!
44. Place plate back on magnet and let sit for - 1 min.
45. Label a new 96-well plate: WTA, experiment description, name/date. Subsequent plates (i.e. NDP and NTA)
should have consistent nomenclature.
46. Transfer supernatant into WTA plate.
For 20 pl of solution, collect 19 pli to avoid taking beads. Discard the original plate. It is okay if some
residual SPRI beads are carried over though every time we take solution from WTA plate we
leave the WTA plate on a magnet.
47. A second SPRI clean up may be helpful to perform at this step if primer dimers are a big issue.
The presence of primer dimers increases the detected DNA concentration by Qubit and will result
in us under loading the amount of CDNA in the Nextera portion (i.e. we will think we have more
CDNA than we do in reality and thereby load less in the Nextera plate). Primer dimers may "soak
up" Tn5 during tagmentation and also reduce the effective concentration of Tn5 used to shear
CDNA molecules from MRNA transcripts this can produce a spiky, hedgehog pattern in the final
Nextera library fragment distribution trace.

WTA QC 1. - Qubit
To obtain CDNA concentrations for a 96-well plate using the Qubit assay, each plate will be combined into a 384-well
opaque plate. The 384-well plate is divided into 4 quadrants, and up to 3. X 96-well plates can be assayed at once (the
standard curve is placed in the 4th quadrant.
48. Prepare standard curve in 6 X 200 pl PCR tubes:
a. Tube 1:: 31 ul of Standard 2
b. Tube 2: 3 ul of TE + 31 pl of Standard 2
C. Tube 3:: 3 pl of TE + 3 pl of Tube 2
d. Tube 4: 31 ul of TE + 3 pl of Tube 3
e. Tube 5: 3 pl of TE + 3 pl of Tube 4
f. Tube 6: 3 pl of Standard 1
49. For each 96-well plate to assay, mix 4.8 ml of buffer with 24 pl of dye. Vortex well and dispense into trough.
50. Pipette 39 pl of buffer/dye into 384-well plate.
51. With WTA plate on magnet, add 1 pl of sample to each well in 384-well plate.
52. Seal tightly, vortex, spin down, and run assay in plate reader at 485 nm / 528 nm.
WTA QC 2. I Bioanalyzer
Run a range of samples (based on their qubit concentrations) on the bioanalyzer. Ensure that the samples loaded on
the bioanalyzer are < 5 ng/ul for consistent results, and dilute in TE if necessary. Speaking with Agilent Technologies,
the bioanalyzer dye does not need to be warmed up to room temperature prior to use (note: they state to do SO in
their protocol because their gDNA bioanalyzer chip does require warming up and they wanted to keep the preparation
instructions fixed across their different methods).
Dilute WTA plate
Use the robot to dilute WTA samples to 0.15 - 0.2 ng/ul in TE. Typically, we add 3 pl of sample into a variable
amount of TE to bring the final concentration to 0.2 ng/ul. Prepare this diluted portion in a newly labeled plate, NDP
(Nextera dilution plate). Save this plate after the experiment is completed, although if freezer space is limiting you
may discard the NDP.
Library Preparation 1 - Tagmentation
At this point, we have amplified CDNA fragments that are about 2 kb length. We will use a hyperactive transposase
enzyme (Tn5) to fragment the CDNA into 300-1000 bp fragments. The Tn5 simultaneously adds a pair of universal
"tag" sequences to each end oft the fragment (except for the 5'and 3' ends, as one of these ends is not knicked;
these ends will inevitably be lost). Our Nextera XT protocol is done in a 384-well plate because our reaction volumes
are 1/8th the volume recommended by Illumina. 41 plates will be processed in this protocol, and each plate is mapped
to either quadrant 1, 2, 3, or 4.
53. Label a new 384-well plate as NTA (Nextera plate).
54. Prepare tagmentation master mix by mixing 600 pl of TD Buffer with 300 pl of ATM.
55. Dispense 73 pl of master mix across 12-well snap-strip PCR tubes.
56. Using a multi-dispense multi-channel pipette, add 1.87 pl of master mix to every well in 384-well plate.
57. Add 0.63 pl of sample to each well in 384-well plate.

62. At RT, immediately add 0.63 pl of NT Buffer. Gently mix, spin down, and incubate at RT for 5 min.
The transposase enzyme cuts and stays attached to DNA, unlike typical restriction enzymes that
migrate and cut different sites over time. To remove Tn5 aftert tagmentation, NT Buffer containing
SDS detergent is added to strip off the Tn5 from DNA. NT Buffer is stored at RT.
Library Preparation 2 - Indexing and low-cycle amplification
After tagmentation, all of our digested CDNA fragments have two universal tag-priming regions. We will amplify our
tagments with a primer containing (1) the priming region complementary to the universal tags, (2) a unique index nt
sequence "barcode", and (3) "adaptors' that complementary hybridize to surface oligos on the Illumina flow cell
(called the P5 orl P7 adaptors). Note that only the P5 end binds to its complementary oligo on the flow cell at the start
of sequencing.
63. Thaw pre-aliquoted plate of nextera barcodes at RT. Spin down prior to opening.
To minimize contamination in barcodes, open plate only mmediately prior to use and seal plate
directly after use.
64. At RT, add 1.25 ul of barcodes mix (i.e. adding 0.63 pl, or 10 pM, of each i5 and i7 index) to NTA plate.
65. Dispense 78 pl of NPM (Nextera PCR Master Mix) across 12-well snap-strip PCR tubes.
66. Add 1.88 pl ofl NPM to each sample and gently mix. Spin plate down.
